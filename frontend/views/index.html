Business Plan

Investment

Initially, REAL will rely on state funding, corresponding to bid values, rather than seeking external investors. As a non-profit entity, our business model focuses on mediating between students and the government. The founding members will create the website, initiate promotion, beginning with IFSP (Federal Institute of Education, Science, and Technology of São Paulo), and later expand to other educational institutions, municipalities, states, and eventually, countries.

Opportunity

REAL sees a significant opportunity in the municipal bidding market, aiming to address local issues while integrating young talents into the workforce. By focusing initially on municipal bids, we aim not only to achieve economic recognition but also to become a reference for public and private companies seeking specialized labor. This strategic approach will help us build a strong customer base and expand our operations sustainably, fostering economic and social development in our community and beyond.

Market and Competition

The current market for municipal bids presents untapped opportunities, with many remaining incomplete due to a lack of specialized resources. Bids for city infrastructure often require competencies taught in courses such as civil engineering, and swift completion can improve the city's structure. These gaps between market demand and specialized skills offer a significant opportunity for REAL and its students. In this competitive environment, existing players pose a challenge, but REAL can differentiate itself by harnessing the energy and capability of college students to offer innovative solutions to municipal government demands.

Issues to be tackled

What we will face

The issue of unfinished bids directly impacts the city, leaving essential projects and services on hold and hindering local progress and development. For instance, the lack of completion of bids for city infrastructure leads to adverse effects such as delays in construction and inadequate services.

Tools to solve them

What we will solve

REAL connects students and government through optimized websites, efficiently completing unfinished bids to benefit the city and residents, while also enhancing infrastructure, providing valuable student learning opportunities, fostering community growth, and promoting collaboration for sustainable development.

Any questions?

REAL implements a rigorous vetting process for both students and projects. Students must demonstrate relevant skills and undergo training, while projects are carefully assessed to ensure feasibility and alignment with student capabilities. Additionally, ongoing supervision and support are provided throughout project execution to maintain quality standards.

REAL employs a transparent project allocation system based on merit and suitability. Projects are matched with students based on their skills, experience, and interests, ensuring a fair and equitable distribution. Furthermore, all project assignments and progress are documented and accessible to stakeholders, promoting transparency in the allocation process.

REAL establishes clear communication channels and dispute resolution mechanisms to address conflicts promptly and effectively. Students are encouraged to maintain open communication with project stakeholders and seek guidance from REAL's support team when encountering challenges. Additionally, mediation and arbitration processes are available to resolve disputes amicably and ensure project continuity.
